How E.coli K1 traverses the blood-brain barrier without arrested by lysosome

Advisor: Associate Prof. Zhihui Cheng

12/2020-07/2021

E.coli K1 is the globally most common cause of neonatal bacterial meningitis. Though many studies revealed the possible mechanisms used by E.coli K1 to traverse the blood-brain barrier, it is still unclear how the intracellular bacteria containing vesicles escape fusion with lysosome. In this study, we focus on several proteins mediating fusion of vesicles and use RNAi as a main method.
